Lockdown Restriction to Citizen Daily Activity Frequency Changes

Countries around the world are in uncharted territory. The emergence and spread of COVID-19 has touched every facet of society. The Spain nationwide lockdown began at March 15, as a result, daily life comes to a halt. To be specific, public gathering party are canceled, employees were sent back to home. All sizes of business are facing many uncertainties about their prospects.

The following Figure 1 presents the changes of different activities regarding the lockdown restrictions. Up to now, stage four restrictions happened twice since the beginning of lockdown at March 13. Stage four was an advanced phase, in which the restriction was limited. More than 23,000 people have died in Spain after contracting coronavirus, making the country one of the worst affected in the world by the illness. At this period, thousands of employee were forced to work at home during nearly three month coronavirus lockdown. Therefore, frequency that people visited workplaces has dropped about 50% compare with before. After the fourth stage lockdown restriction ended, employees allowed to work in the physical workplace and frequency that people visit workplaces increased, still lower than the average rate. Remote working caused by coronavirus has made many people aware of the convenience. Besides, Spain government are protecting the rights of remote workers, such as distance working must be a voluntary choice or employees have the right to apply compensation for any direct or indirect costs related to remote work ELPAIS(ELPAIS 2020).

Figure 1: The frequency that people visit workplaces and residential relative to the baseline period Jan 3 - Feb 6, 2020. Since the outbreak of COVID-19, frequency that people visit residentials was increase dramatically and decreased when the fourth stage lockdown restriction finished. In contrast, the frequency that people visit workplaces decrease significantly until the fourth stage lockdown restriction finished.

Within the lockdown period, parks remain closed. Therefore, runners and cyclists take the broad avenue as a racetrack. Most of them transfer the outdoor sports to exercise at home. In Figure 2, people rarely visit parks during the lockdown period. At the end of April, it has seen a steadily declining number of deaths per day, leading to the country’s plan to broaden lockdown restrictions. Spain has released an exit strategy from the lockdown restriction in a bid to return the normality, while keeping COVID-19 under control euronews(euronews 2020). With the gradual relaxation of the lockdown restriction, retail and recreation industry allowed to re-open to the public, such as sidewalk café reopened and Spaniards can exercise outdoors CNN(Goodman 2020). As a consequence, frequency that people visit parks increase over than 50%.
